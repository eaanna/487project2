Where do bears live?

Bears live in all regions and landscapes of North America, from the Appalachian Mountains in the East to the Rockies in the West.

This map, created from three seperate maps for each species found in North America, shows the current distribution of bears across the continent. Historically, bear populations have been shrinking, including the endangered Polar Bear and the Grizzly Bear. However, Black and Brown Bears as a whole have made a comeback within recent decades.

Be Bear-y Aware

Black and Brown bears aren't endangered, come to humans for food

Bear management is one of the most important aspects of saving the bear populations in North America. National groups, such as the National Park Service or the US Fish and Wildlife Service, emphasize how important it is to be educated about the furry creatures. Education about bear conservation and prevention of human-bear interaction is essential to future bear survival in North America.
